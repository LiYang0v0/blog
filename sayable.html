語言的魅力 The Enchantment of Language

他們訴諸語言並非旨在改造這個世界，而且深知個人無能為力卻還言說，這便是語言擁有的魅力。

— 高行健，〈文學的理由〉，2000

They turned to language not in order to remake the world. Fully aware of the powerlessness of the individual, they nevertheless continued to speak. Therein lies the enchantment of language.

— Gao Xingjian, “The Case for Literature,” 2000

作家的第三隻眼 The Writer’s Third Eye

再說，抒情也有許許多多的層次，更高的境界不如冷眼靜觀。詩意便隱藏在這有距離的觀注中。而這觀注的目光如果也審視作家本人，同樣凌駕於書中的人物和作者之上，成為作家的第三隻眼，一個盡可能中性的目光，那麼災難與人世的垃圾便也經得起端詳，在勾起痛苦、厭惡與噁心的同時，也喚醒悲憫、對生命的愛惜與眷戀之情。

— 高行健，〈文學的理由〉，2000

Lyricism, moreover, has many levels. At its higher reaches, it gives way to a cool and detached gaze. Poetry lies hidden within this distanced act of observation. And when that observing gaze also turns upon the writer—rising above both the characters in the book and the author himself, and becoming the writer’s third eye, a gaze as neutral as possible—then even catastrophe and the refuse of the human world can bear close scrutiny. While arousing pain, revulsion, and nausea, they also awaken compassion, a cherishing of life, and a deep attachment to it.

— Gao Xingjian, “The Case for Literature,” 2000
